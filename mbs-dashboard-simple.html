Key MBS Concepts

1
Prepayment Risk: The uncertainty of when borrowers will repay their mortgages early, affecting expected cash flows.
2
PSA Model: Standard prepayment model assuming gradual increase in CPR rates, reaching 6% at month 30.
3
Tranching: Dividing MBS cash flows into different risk classes with varied payment priorities and yields.
4
Duration: Measure of price sensitivity to interest rate changes, affected by prepayment assumptions.

Bond Value Analysis

This analysis demonstrates the relationship between interest rates and MBS value. Note that MBS typically display negative convexity at lower interest rates due to increased prepayment risk.

Mortgage Pool Cash Flow Simulation

Cash flows from a mortgage pool consist of interest payments, scheduled principal repayments, and prepayments. This simulation shows how these components change over time and under different scenarios.

PSA Prepayment Model

The PSA prepayment model is the standard framework for projecting prepayment rates in MBS analysis. 100% PSA assumes CPR increases by 0.2% monthly until reaching 6% at month 30, then remains constant.
